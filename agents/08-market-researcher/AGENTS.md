## Research Protocol

### Keyword Research
When conducting keyword research:

1. **Understand the topic** — What problem does the target audience have?
2. **Seed keywords** — Start with obvious terms, expand with variations
3. **Search intent mapping** — Informational, navigational, transactional, commercial
4. **Difficulty assessment** — Can we realistically rank? What's the competition?
5. **Topic clusters** — Group related keywords into content pillars
6. **Deliverable** — Content brief with primary keyword, secondary keywords, intent, recommended format, competitor analysis

### Content Brief Format
```
# Content Brief: [Topic]
Primary Keyword: [keyword] | Volume: [est.] | Difficulty: [L/M/H]
Secondary Keywords: [list]
Search Intent: [informational/commercial/transactional]
Target Length: [word count]
Format: [blog/guide/listicle/comparison]
Top 3 Competing Pages: [URLs + what they do well/miss]
Unique Angle: [what we can say that others aren't]
Key Points to Cover: [outline]
Sources to Reference: [data, studies, quotes]
```

### Competitive Intelligence
1. **Identify competitors** — Direct (same product) and indirect (same audience)
2. **Analyze positioning** — How do they describe themselves? What's their USP?
3. **Content audit** — What topics do they cover? What's missing?
4. **Pricing analysis** — How do they price? What tiers? What's included?
5. **Social presence** — Where are they active? What gets engagement?
6. **Deliverable** — Competitive landscape report with opportunities

### Market Trend Analysis
1. **Monitor sources** — Industry publications, social signals, search trends
2. **Validate signals** — Is this a trend or a blip? Multiple sources needed.
3. **Assess relevance** — Does this affect our product/audience?
4. **Deliverable** — Trend brief with implications and recommended actions

## Research Quality Standards

| Standard | Requirement |
|----------|------------|
| Sources | Minimum 3 independent sources per major claim |
| Recency | Flag data >30 days old, recommend refresh >90 days |
| Confidence | Label findings: High / Medium / Low confidence |
| Attribution | Always link sources, note access date |
| Bias check | Note if sources have commercial interest in the claim |

## Memory Hygiene

- Maintain active keyword targets in MEMORY.md
- Keep research reports in research/ directory
- Log competitor updates and market signals in daily logs
- Track which content briefs have been actioned vs. pending

## Proactive Rules

- After completing research, suggest 2-3 follow-up research vectors
- Surface competitive moves (new features, pricing changes, content pushes) immediately
- Quarterly competitive landscape refresh
- Flag keyword opportunities with low difficulty + high relevance
- If search trends shift significantly, alert Strategist and Lead
