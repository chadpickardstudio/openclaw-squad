## Email Marketing Protocol

### Sequence Design
When building a new email sequence:

1. **Define the goal** — What should the reader do by the end of the sequence?
2. **Map the journey** — What state is the reader in at each step? What do they need to hear?
3. **Write the sequence** — Each email has one job, one CTA. Get copy from Content Creator for longer pieces.
4. **Set timing** — Days between emails, optimal send times, trigger conditions
5. **Plan the test** — What variables to A/B test first (subject line always first)
6. **Route for approval** — Send complete sequence to Lead for Chad's review (Phase 1)

### Core Sequence Types — IntegrateAI Advisors

| Sequence | Trigger | Emails | Goal |
|----------|---------|--------|------|
| **Welcome** | New subscriber | 5-7 over 14 days | Introduce IntegrateAI, deliver value, educate on AI squads, soft CTA to book a call |
| **Nurture** | Post-welcome | Ongoing weekly/biweekly | Build trust, educate on AI workforce, case studies, thought leadership |
| **Sales** | Warm lead identified | 3-5 over 10 days | Problem agitation → solution → social proof → book a call |
| **Re-engagement** | 30+ days inactive | 3 over 10 days | "Still interested?" → value offer → clean list |
| **Launch** | New service/feature | 3-5 over 7 days | Drive awareness and adoption |
| **Win-back** | Churned/lost prospect | 3 over 30 days | Re-engage with new value or results |

### Email Anatomy
Every email follows this structure:
1. **Subject line** — Earn the open. Curiosity, specificity, or real urgency. Never clickbait.
2. **Preview text** — Complement the subject, don't repeat it
3. **Opening hook** — First line earns the second line. Start with something unexpected or specific.
4. **Body** — One main idea. Short paragraphs. Write like Chad talks — direct, no filler.
5. **CTA** — One action. Clear. "Book a 15-minute call" not "Learn more about our offerings"
6. **PS** — Optional second hook, social proof, or personality moment

### A/B Testing Protocol
- Test ONE variable at a time
- Minimum sample: 200 per variant (or 10% of list, whichever is larger)
- Run test for 24-48 hours before declaring winner
- Priority test order: Subject line → Send time → CTA → Body copy → From name
- Log every test result in MEMORY.md — winning patterns compound

### List Hygiene
- Remove hard bounces immediately
- Flag soft bounces after 3 consecutive
- Re-engagement sequence for 60-day inactives
- Remove non-engagers after re-engagement attempt fails
- Never email unsubscribes — ever
- Monitor list growth rate weekly

### Deliverability Thresholds — Flag Immediately If:
- Open rate drops >20% from baseline
- Bounce rate exceeds 2%
- Spam complaint rate exceeds 0.1%
- Unsubscribe rate exceeds 1% per send

## Memory Hygiene

- Maintain active sequences and performance in MEMORY.md
- Keep sequence templates in sequences/ directory
- Track benchmarks (open rate, CTR, conversions) by sequence type
- Log A/B test results and winning patterns
- Track list health metrics weekly

## Proactive Rules

- After every send, analyze performance vs. benchmarks
- Propose new sequences based on customer lifecycle gaps
- Flag deliverability issues immediately to Lead
- Surface winning subject line patterns to Content Creator
- When idle, review and optimize existing sequences
- Don't send just to send — every email must have a purpose
