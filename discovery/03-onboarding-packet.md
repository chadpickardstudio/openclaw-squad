## How to Talk to Your Squad

You talk to **one person: the Lead**. Think of the Lead as your chief of staff. You give it direction, and it manages the team.

### The Right Way to Communicate

**Give context, not commands.**

| Instead of this... | Say this... |
|--------------------|-------------|
| "Send 50 cold emails" | "We need more leads for [service]. Can you work on outreach?" |
| "Post on Instagram at 3pm" | "We need to be more active on social. Our audience is [description]." |
| "Write a 1000-word blog post about X" | "We should create content about [topic]. Our customers care about [thing]." |

The Lead will figure out the best approach, assign the right team member, and come back with results or questions.

### When the Lead Asks You Questions — Answer Them

Your squad is smart, but it's new to your business. In the first few weeks, the Lead will ask clarifying questions. This is a good sign — it means the squad is calibrating to your specific needs. The more context you give early on, the faster the squad becomes fully autonomous.

### If Something Is Urgent, Say So

The squad processes requests in priority order. If something is time-sensitive, say: "This is urgent — [reason]." The Lead will prioritise accordingly.

## What to Expect

### Week 1: Learning Phase

The squad is getting to know your business. Expect:
- Output quality around **70%** — good but not perfect
- Questions from the Lead about your preferences
- The squad may get your brand voice slightly wrong at first
- **Your input matters most this week.** Feedback now saves months of correction later.

### Weeks 2–4: Calibration Phase

Quality improves to around **85%**. The squad is learning your patterns:
- Fewer questions, more proactive action
- Content starts matching your voice
- Sales outreach becomes more personalised
- The squad begins making suggestions

### Month 2+: Autonomous Phase

Quality reaches **90%+**. The squad is fully operational:
- Proactive suggestions (not just reactive task completion)
- Agents improving their own processes
- The Lead identifies opportunities you haven't asked about
- Minimal input needed from you

## Your Responsibilities

Your squad handles the work. You handle three things:

### 1. Approve High-Impact Actions

Some actions are too important for the squad to take alone. When the Lead asks for approval, it's because the action involves:
- **Spending money** (processing payments, purchasing subscriptions)
- **Legal commitments** (signing agreements, accepting terms)
- **Bulk actions** (mass emails, bulk updates)
- **Account changes** (modifying passwords, permissions)

The Lead will explain what it wants to do and why. You approve or deny.

### 2. Give Feedback

If the squad produces something that doesn't match your expectations:
- Tell the Lead what's wrong and what you'd prefer
- Be specific: "This blog post is too formal, we're more casual" is better than "I don't like it"
- The squad learns from every correction

### 3. Keep Credentials Current

Your squad connects to your business tools ([list connected tools]). If a password or API key changes, the squad will alert you. Update it promptly so the team can keep working.

## FAQ

**Q: Can the squad access my email/social/CRM without my knowledge?**
A: The squad only accesses tools you explicitly connected during setup. It cannot access anything else. All actions are logged.

**Q: What if I want to add a new tool?**
A: Tell the Lead or contact us. We'll connect the new tool and configure the appropriate agent to use it.

**Q: Can the squad make mistakes?**
A: Yes, especially in the first few weeks. That's why high-impact actions require your approval. For lower-impact tasks (drafting content, researching competitors), the squad operates autonomously but you can always provide feedback.

**Q: What happens to my data?**
A: Your data stays on your infrastructure. [If self-hosted: "The squad runs on your own server — nothing leaves your environment." If hosted: "Your data is stored securely on our managed servers with encryption at rest and in transit. We do not share your data with anyone."]

**Q: Can I talk to individual agents directly?**
A: You can, but we recommend going through the Lead. The Lead knows the full context and will route your request to the right team member. Talking to agents directly bypasses the coordination layer.

**Q: What if I go on holiday?**
A: The squad keeps working. It will queue any approval requests for when you return. For extended absences, you can designate someone else to handle approvals, or we can adjust the squad to operate more conservatively.
