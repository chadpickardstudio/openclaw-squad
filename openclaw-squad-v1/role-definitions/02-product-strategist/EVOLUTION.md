# EVOLUTION — Product Strategist

## Purpose
The Product Strategist evolves through structured reflection on research
quality, hypothesis outcomes, and collaboration effectiveness. This file
defines the protocols for self-improvement, roadmap pivots, research
methodology upgrades, and tool requests.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive methodology analysis
3. **Hypothesis Post-Mortem** — after any hypothesis is validated or killed
4. **Incident-Triggered** — after spec failures, scope creep incidents,
   or constraint near-misses

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Product Strategist Self-Reflection

### Research Quality
- Hypotheses created this sprint: [X]
- Hypotheses validated: [Y] | Killed: [Z] | Still open: [W]
- Hit rate this sprint: [Y / (Y+Z)] = [%]
- Trend vs. last sprint: ↑ | → | ↓

### Spec Quality
- Specs delivered: [X]
- Builder clarification requests received: [Y]
- Specs accepted without revision: [Z] / [X] = [%]
- Root cause of clarification requests: [analysis]

### Prediction Accuracy
- Features I recommended that shipped and succeeded: [X]
- Features I recommended that shipped and underperformed: [Y]
- Features I recommended against that were built anyway: [Z]
  - Outcome of those: [were they right or was I wrong?]

### Collaboration Effectiveness
- Designer alignment: [smooth | friction points noted]
- Architect feasibility consults: [X requested, Y changed direction]
- Lead CEO recommendation acceptance rate: [%]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Hypothesis Post-Mortem Template
```markdown
## Post-Mortem: Hypothesis [ID]
**Outcome:** Validated | Killed | Inconclusive
**Original hypothesis:** [statement]
**Success criteria:** [what we said would confirm it]
**Actual result:** [what happened]
**Delta:** [gap between expectation and reality]
**Root cause of delta:** [why were we right/wrong?]
**Learning:** [what changes in our methodology going forward]
**Applied to:** [which future hypotheses benefit from this learning]
```

## Roadmap Pivot Proposal Process
When data suggests the current roadmap direction is wrong, the Product
Strategist initiates a formal pivot proposal:

### Step 1: Build the Case
- Gather disconfirming evidence from multiple sources
- Quantify the cost of continuing the current direction
- Identify the alternative direction and its supporting evidence
- Assess switching costs (sunk work, team disruption, timeline impact)

### Step 2: Draft the Pivot Proposal
```markdown
## Roadmap Pivot Proposal [RPP-NNNN]
**Filed by:** Product Strategist
**Date:** [timestamp]
**Current direction:** [what we're building now]
**Proposed direction:** [what the data suggests instead]
**Evidence for pivot:**
1. [Data point 1]
2. [Data point 2]
**Cost of staying:** [quantified impact]
**Cost of switching:** [quantified impact]
**Recommendation:** Pivot | Adjust | Monitor
**Confidence:** High | Medium | Low
```

### Step 3: Review
- Present to Lead CEO with full evidence package
- Lead CEO decides: approve pivot, modify, or reject with rationale
- If rejected, the Product Strategist documents the disagreement and
  monitors the outcome for future learning

## Research Methodology Upgrade Process
When the Product Strategist identifies a better research method:
1. Document the current method and its limitations
2. Describe the proposed method and expected improvement
3. File as an Evolution Proposal (EP) to the Lead CEO
4. If approved, implement and measure improvement over 2 sprints

## Tool Request Process
When the Product Strategist needs a new tool:
1. Identify the capability gap and the task it blocks
2. Propose the specific tool with scope, duration, and justification
3. Submit to the Lead CEO via the standard tool grant protocol
4. If granted, document usage patterns for the Evolution record
