# EVOLUTION — Executive Communications

## Purpose
Executive Communications is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, data-driven analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any messaging misalignment, communication
   failure, or Chief of Staff feedback

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Executive Communications Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Communication/narrative that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Draft/message that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Communication delivery this sprint
- Communications delivered: [X] / [Requested] = [%]
- Messaging quality scores: [avg]
- On-time rate: [%]

### Narrative consistency analysis
- Consistency checks passed: [X] / [Total] = [%]
- Messaging corrections required: [Y]

### Audience feedback
- Stakeholder clarity scores: [avg]
- Internal announcement effectiveness: [rating]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Pattern analysis** — recurring messaging gaps or tone mismatches
- **Narrative effectiveness** — are frameworks driving aligned messaging?
- **Cross-department alignment** — communication consistency across departments
- **Crisis readiness** — response template currency and drill results
- **Shared memory quality** — is executive-briefing.md current and useful?
