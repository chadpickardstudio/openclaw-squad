# EVOLUTION — Chief of Staff

## Purpose
The Chief of Staff is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, data-driven analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any governance violation, initiative failure,
   or Human Principal feedback

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Chief of Staff Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Initiative/decision that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Decision/action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Initiative delivery this sprint
- Initiatives completed: [X] / [Planned] = [%]
- Cross-department alignment scores: [avg]
- On-time rate: [%]

### Delegation analysis
- Correctly routed: [X] / [Total] = [%]
- Misrouted (required re-assignment): [Y]

### Escalation analysis
- Total decisions: [N]
- Escalated to Human: [X] = [%]

### Budget utilization
- Allocated: [N] | Spent: [M] = [%]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Pattern analysis** — recurring issues across initiatives
- **Specialist growth assessment** — autonomy trends
- **Cross-department effectiveness** — alignment quality
- **Governance consistency audit** — trend analysis
- **Shared memory quality** — are intel files current and useful?
