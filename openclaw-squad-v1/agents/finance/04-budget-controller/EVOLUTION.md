# EVOLUTION — Budget Controller

## Purpose
The Budget Controller is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, data-driven analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any budget overrun, policy violation,
   or Finance Lead feedback

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Budget Controller Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Budget decision/process that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Decision/action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Budget management this sprint
- Budget reports delivered: [X] / [Planned] = [%]
- Budget accuracy (variance): [avg %]
- On-time report delivery: [%]

### Cost optimization
- Savings opportunities identified: [X]
- Savings realized: [Y] = [% of identified]

### Budget request processing
- Requests processed: [N]
- Turnaround time avg: [interaction turns]

### Department compliance
- Departments within budget: [X] / [Total] = [%]
- Departments requiring intervention: [Y]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Pattern analysis** — recurring budget management challenges
- **Forecast accuracy assessment** — projection vs actual trends
- **Cross-department effectiveness** — budget advisory quality
- **Cost optimization tracking** — cumulative savings trends
- **Process efficiency** — request handling improvements
