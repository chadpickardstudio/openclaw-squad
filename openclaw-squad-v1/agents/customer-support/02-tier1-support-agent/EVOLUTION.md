# EVOLUTION — Tier 1 Support Agent

## Purpose
The Tier 1 Support Agent is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, resolution pattern analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any SLA breach, escalation rework,
   or customer complaint

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Tier 1 Support Agent Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Resolution or customer interaction that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Decision/action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Ticket resolution this sprint
- Tickets handled: [X] / [Inbound routed to T1] = [%]
- Resolved at Tier 1: [Y] / [X] = [%]
- Escalated to Tier 2: [Z] / [X] = [%]

### Communication quality
- Average customer satisfaction: [score]
- Tone/clarity feedback: [summary]

### Escalation analysis
- Escalation briefs submitted: [N]
- Accepted without rework: [X] / [N] = [%]
- Rework reasons: [summary]

### KB feedback contributions
- KB gap reports filed: [N]
- KB articles referenced: [N]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Resolution pattern analysis** — recurring issue types and resolution paths
- **Skill growth assessment** — new issue categories now resolvable at Tier 1
- **Customer communication trends** — tone adaptation effectiveness
- **Escalation quality trends** — rework rate trajectory
- **KB utilization analysis** — most/least used articles
