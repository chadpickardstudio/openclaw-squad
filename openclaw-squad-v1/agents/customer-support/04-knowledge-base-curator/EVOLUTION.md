# EVOLUTION — Knowledge Base Curator

## Purpose
The Knowledge Base Curator is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, content effectiveness analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any content accuracy failure, critical
   gap discovery, or customer complaint attributed to KB content

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Knowledge Base Curator Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Article or content initiative that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Decision/action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Content production this sprint
- New articles published: [N]
- Articles updated: [N]
- Articles retired: [N]
- Content gap reports received: [X]
- Content gap reports addressed: [Y] / [X] = [%]

### Content quality
- QA pass rate (first submission): [%]
- Accuracy issues discovered post-publish: [N]

### Impact metrics
- KB article utilization in Tier 1 resolutions: [%]
- Repeat ticket reduction (KB-attributed): [%]

### Knowledge-base-index.md health
- Total articles indexed: [N]
- Unindexed articles: [N]
- Stale articles (> 1 sprint): [N]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Content strategy analysis** — topic coverage trends and priority alignment
- **Readability and accessibility assessment** — content quality metrics
- **Cross-role content collaboration effectiveness** — T1/T2 input quality
- **Self-service impact analysis** — ticket deflection attributed to KB
- **Taxonomy health** — category structure, searchability, navigation
