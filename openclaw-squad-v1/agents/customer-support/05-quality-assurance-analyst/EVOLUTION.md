# EVOLUTION — Quality Assurance Analyst

## Purpose
The Quality Assurance Analyst is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, quality trend analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any CSAT failure, missed quality issue
   reaching a customer, or quality standard dispute

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Quality Assurance Analyst Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Review or quality initiative that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Decision/action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Review activity this sprint
- Tickets reviewed: [X]
- Approved on first pass: [Y] / [X] = [%]
- Returned for rework: [Z] / [X] = [%]
- KB articles reviewed: [N]

### CSAT analysis
- Average CSAT score: [value] vs [target]
- CSAT trend: Improving | Stable | Declining
- Quality-driven improvements identified: [N]

### Process improvements
- Proposals submitted: [N]
- Proposals accepted: [X]
- Impact of implemented improvements: [summary]

### Review quality
- Review turnaround time: [avg]
- Feedback actionability: [agent improvement rate]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Quality trend deep-dive** — multi-sprint quality patterns and root causes
- **Review process effectiveness** — are reviews catching the right issues?
- **Squad quality culture assessment** — is quality mindset improving?
- **Standards relevance check** — do current standards reflect actual quality needs?
- **Cross-role feedback effectiveness** — is feedback translating to improvement?
