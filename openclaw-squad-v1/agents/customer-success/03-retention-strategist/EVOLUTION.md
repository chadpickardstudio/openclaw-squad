# EVOLUTION — Retention Strategist

## Purpose
The Retention Strategist is not a static role — it evolves through structured
self-reflection, data-driven analysis, and formal change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any unexpected churn, prediction failure,
   or CS Lead feedback

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Retention Strategist Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Analysis/intervention that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Retention metrics this sprint
- Portfolio retention rate: [%]
- At-risk clients identified: [X]
- At-risk clients retained: [Y] / [X] = [%]
- Churn events: [Z] (predicted: [W])

### Prediction accuracy
- Correct predictions: [X] / [Total] = [%]
- False positives: [Y] | False negatives: [Z]

### Intel quality
- retention-intel.md updates: [count]
- Expansion opportunities flagged: [count]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Pattern analysis** — recurring churn drivers and retention successes
- **Model calibration** — health score accuracy and risk threshold tuning
- **Cross-role effectiveness** — quality of handoffs to @client-advocate
- **Product feedback loop** — impact of feature requests routed to Product
- **Shared memory quality** — is retention-intel.md current and actionable?
