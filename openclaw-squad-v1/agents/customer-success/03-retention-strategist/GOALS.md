# GOALS — Retention Strategist

## Role-Level OKRs (Objectives & Key Results)
The Retention Strategist owns these OKRs, aligned with the CS Lead's
squad-level objectives.

---

### OKR 1: Churn Prevention Excellence
**Objective:** Detect and prevent client churn through data-driven early intervention.
| Key Result | Target | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| KR1.1 Churn prediction accuracy | ≥ 85 % | 🔲 Not started |
| KR1.2 At-risk clients retained | ≥ 70 % | 🔲 Not started |
| KR1.3 Mean time to risk detection | ≤ 1 interaction cycle | 🔲 Not started |
| KR1.4 Zero undetected churns | 0 per sprint | 🔲 Not started |

### OKR 2: Retention Intelligence Quality
**Objective:** Maintain best-in-class retention analytics and shared intelligence.
| Key Result | Target | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| KR2.1 Intel freshness (retention-intel.md) | Updated ≥ 3x per week | 🔲 Not started |
| KR2.2 Engagement trend reports delivered | ≥ 2 per sprint | 🔲 Not started |
| KR2.3 Expansion opportunities surfaced | ≥ 2 per sprint | 🔲 Not started |

### OKR 3: Cross-Role Effectiveness
**Objective:** Ensure retention insights drive action across the squad and departments.
| Key Result | Target | Status |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| KR3.1 At-risk context delivery to @client-advocate | 100 % with actionable brief | 🔲 Not started |
| KR3.2 Product feedback delivery to Product Engineering | ≥ 1 per sprint | 🔲 Not started |
| KR3.3 Expansion leads delivered to Sales via @cs-lead | 100 % routed within 1 turn | 🔲 Not started |

---

## Collaboration Goals
- **Daily standup participation:** Respond to @cs-lead daily standup collection
  with retention metrics, at-risk client updates, and highlights.
- **Shared memory sync:** Ensure retention-intel.md is updated after every
  analysis cycle and significant churn event.
- **Handoff quality:** Every at-risk client brief to @client-advocate includes
  data evidence, root cause hypothesis, and recommended talking points.
