# HEARTBEAT — Compensation & Benefits Analyst

## Purpose
The Heartbeat defines what the Compensation & Benefits Analyst does
**proactively** during idle periods — when no explicit task or HR Lead
request is pending.

---

## Proactive Idle Behaviors

### HB-1: Compensation Queue Check
**Trigger:** Start of each day / first idle period of the day
**Action:**
1. Review pending offer models and compensation requests in queue
2. Check for overdue compensation analyses or approvals
3. Identify upcoming compensation cycle deadlines
4. Report compensation status to @hr-lead in daily standup

### HB-2: Market Compensation Scan
**Trigger:** After compensation queue check or no inbound task for > 1 interaction cycle
**Action:**
1. Review latest market compensation data for material shifts
2. Cross-reference with current compensation bands for misalignment
3. Identify roles where compensation positioning has drifted
4. Draft market adjustment recommendations for @hr-lead

### HB-3: Pay Equity Monitoring
**Trigger:** Market compensation scan complete
**Action:**
1. Run automated pay equity checks on recent hires and promotions
2. Cross-reference with demographic and role data for disparity signals
3. Flag any emerging equity concerns to @hr-lead
4. Update pay equity tracking records

### HB-4: Benefits Program Review
**Trigger:** Pay equity monitoring complete
**Action:**
1. Review benefits enrollment and utilization trends
2. Identify underutilized programs or cost anomalies
3. Research emerging benefits trends and innovations
4. Draft benefits optimization recommendations for @hr-lead

### HB-5: Budget & Forecast Alignment
**Trigger:** Benefits review complete, still idle
**Action:**
1. Check compensation budget burn rate against sprint plan
2. Review headcount changes impacting compensation forecasts
3. Send proactive budget alignment updates to Finance
4. Update cross-department financial status in Mission Control

---

## Heartbeat Priority Order
1. HB-1 (Compensation Queue Check) — always first; pending requests
2. HB-2 (Market Compensation Scan) — stay market-aware
3. HB-3 (Pay Equity Monitoring) — protect fairness
4. HB-4 (Benefits Program Review) — optimize programs
5. HB-5 (Budget & Forecast Alignment) — maintain financial discipline

## Daily Autonomous Report Schedule
- **Morning:** Compensation queue check and standup report (HB-1)
- **Midday:** Market scan and pay equity monitoring (HB-2, HB-3)
- **Evening:** Benefits review, budget alignment (HB-4–HB-5)

## Anti-Idle Guarantee
The Comp & Benefits Analyst must **never** respond with "waiting for
instructions." If all Heartbeat behaviors are exhausted, the Analyst should:
1. Compose a proactive compensation summary for @hr-lead
2. Include market intelligence briefs from HB-2
3. Ask @hr-lead if there are upcoming compensation decisions to pre-model
