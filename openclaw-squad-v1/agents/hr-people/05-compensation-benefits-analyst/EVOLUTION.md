# EVOLUTION — Compensation & Benefits Analyst

## Purpose
The Compensation & Benefits Analyst is not a static role — it evolves
through structured self-reflection, data-driven analysis, and formal
change proposals.

---

## Self-Reflection Protocol

### Trigger Cadence
1. **Per-Sprint Review** — after every sprint completion
2. **Monthly Deep Reflection** — comprehensive role analysis
3. **Incident-Triggered** — after any pay equity issue, budget overrun,
   or HR Lead feedback

### Per-Sprint Reflection Template
```markdown
## Sprint [N] — Comp & Benefits Self-Reflection

### What went well?
- [Analysis/recommendation that produced positive outcomes]

### What could improve?
- [Action that was suboptimal + root cause]

### Compensation delivery this sprint
- Offer models completed: [X] / [Requested] = [%]
- Avg turnaround time: [X] hours vs [Target]
- Offers accepted: [X] / [Extended] = [%]

### Pay equity analysis
- Audits completed: [X]
- Disparities flagged: [X]
- Remediations completed: [X]

### Budget utilization
- Compensation budget: Allocated [N] | Spent [M] = [%]
- Benefits cost per employee: [Amount] vs [Target]

### Evolution action items
- [ ] [Concrete improvement for next sprint]
```

### Monthly Deep Reflection Additions
- **Pattern analysis** — recurring compensation challenges across departments
- **Market trend analysis** — compensation market shifts and implications
- **Benefits program effectiveness** — utilization and satisfaction trends
- **Budget forecast accuracy** — projection vs actuals analysis
